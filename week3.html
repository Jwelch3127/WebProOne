About Me

Life is as extreme as you want to make it.

Hi! My name is James, and I just began my journey as a graduate student in the Learning Design and Technology master’s program at USF. This will be my third degree since 2016. I earned a BA degree in psychology from Stetson University located in historic DeLand, Florida (2016). From there I earned an MS degree in College Student Affairs from Nova Southeastern University located in tropical Fort Lauderdale, FL (2018).

Upon graduating from NSU, I moved to Tampa to work at the University of Tampa. My position there was as a staff assistant for the Center for Teaching and Learning. During the two-year period I was there (2018-2020), my main responsibilities were preparing department budget reports, training Graduate Assistants, coordinating events and other office clerical duties. It was there, that I was introduced to the department of Educational Technology and Learning Design.

Shortly after I left UT, I accepted a position at USF as a Course Coordinator for the Psychology department. It is in my current position that I truly get to practice skills geared toward learning design. My end goal would be to continue working at USF and eventually become a full Learning Designer helping to create engaging content that strengthens student learning and success. Currently I am located in Zephyrhills where I live with my wife of 5 years and three dogs [a Jack Russell Terrier-mix, a Lab-mix, and a Siberian Husky]. I definitely keep a busy schedule and identify as a "life-long-learner," as I enjoy learning everything I can about a variety of different subjects.
